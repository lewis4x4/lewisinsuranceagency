## Understanding Florida's Auto Insurance Rebate Requirement: Background and Context

Progressive Insurance's announcement that it will return nearly $1 billion to Florida auto policyholders represents the practical application of Florida's consumer protection laws governing insurance company profits. Florida Statute requires auto insurers to return excess profits to policyholders if their underwriting gains exceed anticipated profit plus 5% of earned premium over any three-calendar-year period. This regulation exists to prevent insurers from earning excessive profits at the expense of Florida consumers while still allowing companies to maintain healthy financial positions.

The history of insurance profit regulation in Florida dates back to legislative efforts to balance consumer protection with market stability. Following periods of significant rate increases that burdened Florida drivers, state lawmakers established profit caps to ensure that when market conditions improve and claims decrease, the financial benefits flow back to policyholders rather than accumulating entirely as corporate profits. This regulatory framework established the foundation for how auto insurers operate in Florida today, requiring companies like Progressive to monitor their three-year rolling profitability and issue credits when thresholds are exceeded.

Under current Florida law, the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation (OIR) oversees compliance with profit cap requirements and communicates proactively with auto insurance companies to ensure credits owed to policyholders are paid. Commissioner Mike Yaworsky stated in October 2025 that "OIR has been diligent in communication with auto insurance companies to proactively ensure credits owed to policyholders are paid, and millions of consumers will start feeling the benefits with many companies in the process of issuing refunds." For drivers in Columbia County and surrounding North Florida areas, this regulatory oversight ensures they receive the same protections as policyholders throughout the state.

## Current Situation: 2025-2026 Developments

### Progressive's Billion-Dollar Rebate Announcement

Progressive Insurance announced in October 2025 that it would return nearly $1 billion to Florida auto policyholders, making this the largest single insurance rebate in Florida history. According to Progressive's Q3 2025 earnings report filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the company recorded a $950 million policyholder credit expense despite posting $2.6 billion in third-quarter net income. Progressive explicitly stated in their earnings report that "despite actions to lower rates in the last year, it is probable that our personal auto profit in Florida for the 2023 to 2025 period will exceed the statutory profit limit that a Florida statute imposes on the profit that any insurance group can earn on personal auto insurance over any three-calendar-year period."

Governor Ron DeSantis announced the rebate during a press conference in Sarasota on October 22, 2025, alongside Florida Insurance Commissioner Mike Yaworsky. The Governor's Office press release emphasized that this rebate demonstrates the success of Florida's insurance reform efforts, stating that "Florida's insurance market continues to see great stability and the strong underwriting gains in the auto market is just the latest sign of success since Florida's historic tort reforms." The announcement highlighted that approximately 2.7 million Progressive personal auto policyholders in Florida would receive credits averaging about $300 per customer.

### Recent Legislative and Regulatory Changes

The Progressive rebate is a direct consequence of Florida's comprehensive insurance reforms enacted in 2022 and 2023. Senate Bill 2A, signed into law in December 2022, eliminated one-way attorney fees for property insurance claims and ended assignment of benefits (AOB) agreements that had contributed to excessive litigation. House Bill 837, signed in March 2023, extended these reforms by transitioning Florida from pure to modified comparative negligence, shortening the statute of limitations for negligence claims from four years to two years, and eliminating one-way attorney fees in most insurance cases. These legislative changes fundamentally altered the litigation landscape in Florida, reducing the incentives for frivolous lawsuits that had driven up insurance costs for years.

### Market Conditions in Florida

Florida's auto insurance market has undergone a dramatic transformation since the 2022-2023 reforms took effect. According to data from the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation, Florida's personal auto incurred loss ratio dropped to 57.5% in 2024, representing the nation's fifth-lowest ratio and a significant improvement from 73.2% in 2023 and 89.7% in 2022. This improvement in loss ratios indicates that insurers are paying out a smaller percentage of premiums in claims, which directly contributes to improved profitability and creates the conditions for both rebates and rate reductions.

## Impact Analysis: Who Is Affected and How

### Impact on Florida Auto Insurance Policyholders

Florida drivers who hold Progressive auto insurance policies will directly benefit from the $1 billion rebate, receiving credits that average approximately $300 per policyholder. According to Progressive's announcement, the actual credit amount for each policyholder will vary in proportion to each auto policy's cost during calendar year 2025, meaning customers who paid higher premiums will receive larger credits. The credits will be applied automatically in early 2026, and no application or action is required from policyholders to receive their rebate.

Beyond Progressive customers, all Florida auto insurance consumers benefit from the improved market conditions that made this rebate possible. According to the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation, GEICO filed a 10.5% rate decrease, State Farm filed a 10.1% decrease with cumulative reductions totaling 20% over the past year, AAA lowered rates by 15% through three separate reductions, USAA filed a 7% decrease, and Florida Farm Bureau filed an 8.7% decrease. Commissioner Yaworsky announced that he has approved 46 filings from over 30 companies for rate decreases in 2025 alone, indicating broad-based relief for Florida drivers regardless of their current insurer.

The competitive pressure created by multiple carriers reducing rates simultaneously benefits consumers by creating a more favorable shopping environment. Florida drivers who compare quotes across multiple insurers are likely to find better rates than they would have received even one or two years ago, when the market was characterized by rate increases averaging 31.7% according to OIR data.

The Progressive rebate signals a fundamental shift in Florida's auto insurance market from crisis mode to competitive stability. ## What Florida Drivers Should Know

### Understanding Your Rebate Eligibility

To qualify for Progressive's Florida rebate, you must have been an active personal auto policyholder with Progressive in Florida as of December 31, 2025. Commercial auto policies are not included in this rebate. If you meet these criteria, you will automatically receive a credit without needing to apply or take any action. The credit will be applied to your account in early 2026 and can be used toward future premium payments or renewals.

### Rate Decreases Beyond the Rebate

In addition to the one-time rebate, Progressive filed an 8.1% average rate decrease with the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation. This means Progressive customers will see both a credit from the rebate and lower ongoing premiums when their policies renew. Combined with similar rate decreases from other major carriers, Florida drivers are entering the most favorable auto insurance pricing environment in years.

### Shopping Your Coverage in the Current Market

The current market conditions create an ideal opportunity for Florida drivers to compare coverage options across multiple insurers. With carriers actively competing for customers through rate decreases, consumers who shop their coverage are likely to find savings. According to industry data, the average Florida driver can expect to see annual premiums decrease by approximately $200-300 beginning in 2025, with some drivers saving significantly more depending on their driving history, vehicle, and coverage levels.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will Progressive send me a check or apply a credit to my account?

According to a Progressive spokesperson clarifying earlier reports, policyholders will receive a credit applied to their account in 2026, rather than a physical check. This credit can be used toward future insurance purchases or premium payments with Progressive. The credit will be applied automatically ‚Äî no action is required on your part to receive it.

### I switched from Progressive to another insurer in 2025. Do I still qualify for the rebate?

Eligibility for the Progressive rebate requires that you had active personal auto coverage with Progressive in Florida as of December 31, 2025. If you switched insurers before that date, you may not qualify for the rebate. Contact Progressive directly with your former policy information to confirm your specific eligibility status.

### Does the rebate affect my current Progressive premium or coverage?

The rebate is a one-time credit separate from your ongoing premium calculations. It does not change your current coverage levels or policy terms. However, Progressive has also filed an 8.1% rate decrease with the Florida OIR, so your premiums at renewal may be lower than your current rates independent of the rebate credit.
