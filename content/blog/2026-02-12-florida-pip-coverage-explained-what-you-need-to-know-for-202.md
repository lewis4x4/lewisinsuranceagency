# Florida PIP Coverage Explained: What You Need to Know for 2026

If you're a Florida driver, understanding Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage has never been more critical. Florida's move away from mandatory PIP coverage represents the biggest change to auto insurance in over 50 years, and these changes will fundamentally reshape how car accident claims work in the Sunshine State.

As residents of Lake City and North Florida prepare for these historic changes, it's essential to understand both current PIP requirements and what's coming in July 2026. Let's break down everything you need to know about Florida PIP coverage and how to protect yourself during this transition.

## What Is Florida PIP Coverage?

Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is Florida's no-fault insurance coverage that has been mandatory since 1971. Personal injury protection, often called "PIP insurance," is a type of car insurance coverage that pays for medical expenses after a crash, regardless of who's at fault. Florida drivers must have $10,000 in PIP insurance, the maximum amount of money (or "limit") your insurance company might pay for injuries you and your passengers sustain after a car accident.

Currently, PIP coverage in Florida provides:

- **80% of medical expenses** up to $10,000 per person
- **60% of lost wages** if you can't work after an accident
- Coverage for essential services like childcare or household help
- Protection regardless of who caused the accident

Florida is a no-fault state, which means drivers must first use their own PIP insurance to pay for medical and other injury-related costs. This system has helped reduce lawsuits and ensured immediate payment of medical bills after accidents.

## The Historic Changes Coming July 1, 2026

The state plans to completely eliminate its Personal Injury Protection (PIP) requirement by July 1, 2026. PIP insurance ends July 1, 2026 - Florida eliminates its $10,000 Personal Injury Protection requirement, replacing it with mandatory bodily injury liability coverage of $25,000/$50,000.

### New Coverage Requirements

Starting July 1, 2026, Florida drivers will need:

1. **Bodily Injury Liability**: $25,000 per person and $50,000 per accident, along with $5,000 in medical payment (MedPay) coverage
2. **Medical Payments (MedPay)**: $5,000 in medical payment (MedPay) coverage
3. **Property Damage Liability**: $10,000 (unchanged from current requirements)

### From No-Fault to At-Fault System

The state will transition from its current "no-fault" system to a "tort" or "at-fault" framework. This change means that Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage will be replaced with mandatory Bodily Injury (BI) liability coverage.

This represents a complete flip in how accidents are handled:
- **Before 2026**: Your own PIP covers your medical bills first, regardless of fault
- **After 2026**: The at-fault driver's insurance becomes primarily responsible for your injuries

## How MedPay Differs from PIP

Understanding the difference between PIP and MedPay is crucial for North Florida drivers preparing for 2026:

### PIP Coverage (Current System)
- Covers 80% of medical expenses up to $10,000
- Includes 60% of lost wages
- Covers essential services
- No-fault coverage

### MedPay Coverage (2026 System)
- MedPay typically covers 100% of reasonable medical expenses up to your policy limits
- **Only covers medical expenses** - no lost wages or services
- Insurance companies must offer at least $5,000 in MedPay coverage, though you can decline this coverage in writing
- Works alongside at-fault system

One of the biggest concerns for Florida drivers is the state's high uninsured motorist rate. In Michigan, New Mexico, Tennessee, Washington and Florida, more than 20% of drivers don't have car insurance. More specifically, as of July 2025, the rate of uninsured motorists in Florida is 5.44% according to the Florida Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles (FLHSMV). ### Why Uninsured Motorist Coverage Becomes Critical

Uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM) coverage becomes increasingly vital. With about 20% of Florida drivers uninsured, UM/UIM coverage protects you if the at-fault driver lacks sufficient insurance to cover your damages.

After July 2026:
- Your health insurance will need to cover your medical bills while fault gets sorted out. Your health insurance becomes your primary protection once PIP ends
- The at-fault driver's bodily injury insurance becomes the primary payment source for your injuries

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens to my current PIP coverage?
Starting July 1, 2026, Florida may eliminate its PIP requirement entirely. After more than 50 years of no-fault insurance, the state could end personal injury protection coverage and moving to a system that works differently for you and your family.

### Will I save money under the new system?
The impact varies by individual circumstances. The impact on premiums is not entirely clear. While some projections suggest potential increases, recent trends show major insurers reducing rates. The actual effect may vary based on individual circumstances and coverage choices.

### How much MedPay coverage should I buy?
Common limits are $2,000, $5,000, $10,000, or $25,000. Many Florida families choose $5,000 or $10,000 for a strong safety net.

### What if I'm hit by an uninsured driver after 2026?
Without adequate UM/UIM coverage, you could be responsible for your own medical bills and vehicle repairs, even if you weren't at fault.
