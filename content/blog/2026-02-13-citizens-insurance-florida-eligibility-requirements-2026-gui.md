## Understanding Citizens Insurance Eligibility Basics

Under Florida law, Citizens may write a new insurance policy only for property that meets one of the following eligibility criteria: Coverage is not available from a Florida-authorized insurance company or premiums for coverage from Florida-authorized insurance companies are more than 20 percent higher than a comparable Citizens policy.

This fundamental requirement ensures Citizens maintains its role as the "insurer of last resort" rather than competing directly with private market insurers. To be eligible for Citizens Insurance, you must live in Florida and be unable to find coverage or have been quoted unaffordable rates through another insurer.

### The Critical 20% Rule

The 20% rule represents one of the most important aspects of Citizens eligibility. You would still be eligible for Citizens coverage if you receive a private market quote that's exactly 20% higher than Citizens. The private market quote must be MORE than 20% higher to disqualify you from Citizens.

This rule has significant implications for North Florida homeowners, particularly in areas like Lake City where private market competition is increasing. For North Florida homeowners in the Lake City area, it's essential to compare Citizens rates with private market options, as the 20% rule could make Citizens coverage unavailable if private options are competitively priced.

## New 2026 Flood Insurance Requirements

Citizens has implemented mandatory flood insurance requirements that directly impact eligibility. Properties over $400,000 require flood coverage as of January 2026, with all properties requiring it by January 2027. These requirements follow a phased implementation schedule that affects different property values at different times.

The flood insurance mandate includes specific requirements:
- Properties in FEMA special flood hazard areas must have flood coverage at policy issuance
- All personal lines residential policyholders must have flood coverage in place for policies effective on or after: January 1, 2024, for a structure that has a dwelling replacement cost of $600,000 or more.
- Properties valued between $400,000 and $500,000 face requirements starting January 1, 2026

The corporation may deny coverage of a personal lines residential risk to an applicant or insured who refuses to secure and maintain flood insurance. This means your willingness and ability to obtain flood insurance can determine your Citizens eligibility.

### Impact on Private Market Options

## Property-Specific Eligibility Requirements

Beyond basic eligibility criteria, Citizens maintains specific property requirements that can affect coverage availability:

### Building Standards and Safety Requirements
Rule 500 E - Submission Procedures – Building Safety or Milestone Inspection Reports has been amended to meet legislative changes, which require Milestone Inspections for certain properties statewide.

Citizens is amending its eligibility rules with approval from the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation (OIR) to make a property ineligible for coverage when authorities have determined it is unsafe for occupancy or it has substantial structural deficiencies.

### Wind Mitigation Requirements
For properties in high-risk wind zones, Citizens may require specific wind mitigation features. Effective January 1, 2009, a personal lines residential structure that is located in the "wind-borne debris region," as defined in s. 1609.2, International Building Code (2006), and that has an insured value on the structure of $750,000 or more is not eligible for coverage by the corporation unless the structure has opening protections as required under the Florida Building Code for a newly constructed residential structure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I apply for Citizens insurance directly online?
No, you must work with an appointed Citizens agent to apply for coverage. Citizens does not accept direct applications from consumers.

### What happens if I'm offered private market coverage while on Citizens?
If a private insurer offers coverage within 20% of your Citizens rate, you'll be required to accept the transfer and cannot remain with Citizens. However, you have the right to shop with other private carriers.

### Do all Florida properties require flood insurance with Citizens?
It depends on your property value and location. Properties over $400,000 require flood coverage as of January 2026, with all properties requiring it by January 2027.

### How long does the Citizens application process take?
Processing times vary based on documentation completeness and underwriting complexity. Working with an experienced agent can help streamline the process and avoid common delays.

### Can I be denied Citizens coverage?
Yes, Citizens can deny coverage if you don't meet eligibility requirements, refuse to obtain required flood insurance, or if your property has significant structural deficiencies or safety issues.
