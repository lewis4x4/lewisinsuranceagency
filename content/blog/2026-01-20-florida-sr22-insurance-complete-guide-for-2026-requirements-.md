# Florida SR-22 Insurance: Complete Guide for 2026 Requirements & Costs

If you've received a notice requiring SR-22 insurance in Florida, you're not alone. This specialized filing affects thousands of Florida drivers annually, particularly those who've experienced license suspensions or serious traffic violations. Understanding your SR-22 requirements is crucial for maintaining your driving privileges and getting back on the road legally.

## What Is SR-22 Insurance in Florida?

An SR-22 is a Certificate of Financial Responsibility. It is filed by your insurer with the Florida Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles (FLHSMV) to verify you carry state-mandated liability coverage. You can't purchase a standalone "SR-22 policy." Instead, the SR-22 filing is added to a compliant auto policy, and your insurer submits it electronically.

Many drivers mistakenly believe SR-22 is a special type of insurance. In reality, SR22 is not insurance itself—it's a state-required filing that proves you carry minimum liability coverage. Think of it as a watchdog system that ensures high-risk drivers maintain continuous insurance coverage.

### Key SR-22 Facts for Florida Drivers:

- **Filing fee**: Generally $15–$25 (one time)
- **Coverage period**: Must maintain coverage for a minimum of three years
- **Electronic filing**: Your insurer submits the SR-22 directly to FLHSMV
- **Immediate notification**: Any lapse triggers automatic suspension

## Who Needs SR-22 Insurance in Florida?

You might need an SR-22 in Florida after certain traffic or legal violations, not including DUIs (which require FR-44s). Some common reasons include: Your license has been revoked or suspended and you need to reinstate it, You've recently been caught driving without insurance or on a suspended license, You've committed serious traffic violations, like a hit-and-run, reckless driving, and other incidents, You've accumulated too many points on your license

### Common Violations Requiring SR-22:

1. **Driving without insurance**
2. **Reckless driving**
3. **Hit-and-run accidents**
4. **Excessive points accumulation**
5. **Suspended license violations**
6. **At-fault accidents without insurance**

### Important Note About DUIs

Unlike most states, Florida requires an FR-44 for DUI convictions. An FR-44 form certifies you hold bodily injury liability coverage of $100,000 per person and $300,000 per accident and property damage liability of $50,000. If you're facing DUI charges, consult with a qualified attorney about potential SR-22 vs. FR-44 requirements.

## Required Coverage Levels

### SR-22 Minimum Requirements

Bodily Injury Liability Insurance: Minimum of $10,000 per person and $20,000 per accident. Property Damage Liability Insurance: Minimum of $10,000. Personal Injury Protection (PIP): At least $10,000.

These coverage amounts represent Florida's standard minimum requirements. In Florida, that's $10,000 property damage liability per crash, and $10,000 PIP per person per crash.

### Important Filing Deadlines

SR22 must be filed within 15 working days of issuance. Missing this deadline can result in additional penalties and extended suspension periods.

## Getting SR-22 Insurance: Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Contact Insurance Providers

Not all insurance companies offer SR-22 filings. Not all insurance companies are willing or able to handle SR-22 filings. Also, many auto insurance companies don't want the hassle of filing SR22s with states and filing SR-26s when policies lapse

### 2. Purchase Qualifying Coverage

Buy an eligible auto policy and ask the insurer to file the SR22 electronically with the state on your behalf. Ensure your policy meets Florida's minimum requirements.

### 3. Maintain Continuous Coverage

It is also the responsibility of the driver to ensure that the form is filed each time the policy renews throughout the duration of the SR22 requirement, which is typically 36 months from the date of suspension. The state does not notify drivers that a renewal date is approaching and failure to maintain a current SR22 certificate will result in automatic suspension of your driver's license.

### 4. Verify Filing Status

After binding, you'll receive electronic ID cards. Many carriers deliver same-day proof for registration or reinstatement.

## Non-Owner SR-22 Policies

If you don't own a vehicle but need SR-22 coverage, non-owner policies provide an affordable solution. A driver who does not have a vehicle registered in their name may purchase a Non-owner policy. Non-owner policies cost far less and carry significantly lower premiums, while still meeting compliance for all SR-22 and FR44 requirements.

### Benefits of Non-Owner SR-22:
- Lower premiums than standard policies
- Covers you when driving borrowed vehicles
- Maintains continuous coverage history
- Satisfies state requirements

## Common SR-22 Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Coverage Lapses

Your insurance company must notify the state immediately, resulting in automatic license suspension. You'll need to obtain new SR-22 coverage and may face additional penalties.

### 2. Insufficient Coverage

Purchasing only the minimum required coverage might save money initially but could leave you financially vulnerable in an accident.

### 3. Ignoring Renewal Dates

If your insurance does lapse, your provider is obligated to notify the DHSMV, which can lead to serious consequences. Consider setting reminders for your payment due dates and reviewing your policy regularly to ensure it meets the minimum coverage requirements.

## SR-22 vs. FR-44: Understanding the Difference

### When FR-44 Is Required

FR-44s, also called Certificates of Higher Limits Liability Coverage, may be used for serious offences such as driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs, while SR-22s are more likely to come into play for traffic violations such as reckless driving or driving without insurance.

### Coverage Differences

FR-44s require drivers to have an increased amount of liability insurance, higher than the state's minimums. It includes the following: $100,000 bodily injury liability per person per accident, $300,000 bodily injury liability per accident total

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long do I need SR-22 insurance in Florida?
Coverage Duration: Maintain SR-22 coverage for 3 years. This period begins from your license suspension date, not when you purchase the policy.

### Can I get SR-22 insurance if I don't own a car?
Yes. You'll need a non-owner SR-22 policy, which provides coverage when driving vehicles you don't own. This is often the most affordable option for drivers who don't regularly drive.

### What happens if I move to another state?
SR-22 requirements typically follow you to your new state. Contact your insurance provider to understand how relocation affects your filing requirements.

### Will my rates ever return to normal?
Yes, but it takes time. Maintaining a clean record for 3-5 years can help restore your rates to near-normal levels. The violation will eventually fall off your driving record, though this process varies by violation type.

### Can I cancel my SR-22 early?
Generally no. The three-year period is set by Florida law and cannot be reduced. However, completing all court requirements and maintaining perfect compliance may help in rare circumstances.
