## Understanding Citizens Insurance Eligibility Requirements

### The 20% Rule: Your Gateway to Citizens Coverage

Under Florida law, Citizens may write a new insurance policy only for property that meets specific eligibility criteria: coverage is not available from a Florida-authorized insurance company, or premiums for coverage from Florida-authorized insurance companies are more than 20 percent higher than Citizens' comparable coverage.

This "20% rule" is the cornerstone of Citizens eligibility. If you receive a quote from a private insurer that's 19% higher than Citizens' quote, you cannot get Citizens coverage. You remain eligible for Citizens only if the private market quote is MORE than 20% higher than Citizens' rates.

For North Florida homeowners in areas like Lake City, this rule has particular significance. The 20% rule could make Citizens coverage unavailable if private options are competitively priced in North Florida markets.

### Property Value Limits That Affect Eligibility

Generally, a personal dwelling must have a replacement cost below $700,000 to be eligible for coverage. However, there are exceptions: Citizens is allowed to insure personal dwellings with a replacement cost of up to $1 million in counties that the Office of Insurance Regulation deems to lack sufficiently competitive insurance options.

These limits ensure Citizens remains focused on properties that truly need coverage through the state program rather than competing with private insurers for high-value homes.

## Mandatory Flood Insurance Requirements

### Phased Implementation Schedule

Properties over $400,000 require flood coverage as of January 2026, with all properties requiring it by January 2027. This represents one of the most significant changes to Citizens eligibility in recent years.

The flood insurance requirement follows this specific timeline:
- **January 2026**: Properties with replacement costs over $400,000
- **January 2027**: All personal residential properties

Citizens may deny coverage to applicants who refuse to secure and maintain flood insurance. This requirement directly impacts eligibility, as unwillingness or inability to obtain flood insurance can disqualify you from Citizens coverage entirely.

### Impact on North Florida Homeowners

While Florida's coastal areas face obvious flood risks, North Florida properties may still fall under flood insurance requirements. Private carriers may not require flood insurance, though lenders can, making private market options potentially more attractive for some homeowners.

For Lake City area homeowners, this creates an interesting dynamic where private insurance might become more appealing if flood insurance requirements make Citizens coverage more expensive or complicated to obtain.

## The Depopulation Program's Impact on Eligibility

### How Depopulation Affects Your Coverage

The Citizens Depopulation Program in 2025 successfully transferred more than 546,000 Citizens policies to private insurance companies approved by the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation. This aggressive depopulation effort continues reshaping the insurance landscape.

If a private carrier's rates are within 20% of Citizens rates, you cannot refuse the transfer and remain with Citizens. This means many current Citizens policyholders will be required to move to private insurers.

### Upcoming Depopulation Activities

Depopulation will continue in 2026, with the Office of Insurance Regulation approving proposals by nine companies to assume policies in February, March and April. These depopulation proposals will likely result in tens of thousands of policies leaving Citizens over a three-month period, with companies like Slide Insurance assuming up to 100,000 policies.

## Who Cannot Get Citizens Insurance

### Criminal History Restrictions

Homeowners previously convicted of insurance fraud or arson within the past decade and a half cannot get coverage through Citizens to help preserve its integrity and financial soundness. This restriction helps protect all policyholders by maintaining the insurer's financial stability.

### Property Condition Requirements

Citizens amended its eligibility rules to make properties ineligible for coverage when authorities determine they are unsafe for occupancy or have substantial structural deficiencies. Properties must meet basic safety and structural standards to qualify for coverage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I apply for Citizens insurance directly online?

No, you must work with an appointed Citizens agent to apply for coverage. Citizens does not accept direct applications from consumers.

### What happens during the depopulation process?

If you receive an offer from a private insurer that's within 20% of your Citizens premium, you must accept the transfer. Customers become ineligible to remain with Citizens if they receive offers within 20% of Citizens premiums - for example, a 19% higher offer would disqualify you from Citizens coverage.

### Do I need flood insurance to maintain Citizens coverage?

Properties over $400,000 require flood coverage as of January 2026, with all properties requiring it by January 2027. Refusing to obtain required flood insurance can result in coverage denial.

### Can I refuse a depopulation transfer?

No, if the private carrier's rates are within 20% of Citizens rates, you cannot refuse the transfer and remain with Citizens.

### What if I have structural issues with my property?

Properties determined unsafe for occupancy or with substantial structural deficiencies are ineligible for Citizens coverage. You must address these issues before applying.
