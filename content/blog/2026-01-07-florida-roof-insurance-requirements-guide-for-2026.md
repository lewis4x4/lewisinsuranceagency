# Florida Roof Insurance Requirements Guide for 2026

Understanding Florida's roof insurance requirements is crucial for every homeowner in the Sunshine State. With home insurance premiums increasing 24% between 2021 and 2024, and Florida homeowners without claims paying $10,384 annually on average, knowing these requirements can help you maintain coverage and potentially reduce costs.

## The 15-Year Roof Rule: What You Need to Know

Florida Statute 627.7011 states that insurers cannot deny or nonrenew a homeowners policy solely because the roof is less than 15 years old. This protection has been a game-changer for many Florida homeowners who previously faced unnecessary roof replacements.

### Key Protection for Newer Roofs

If your roof is under 15 years old, you're legally protected from insurance companies dropping your coverage based solely on age. "We often see people rushing to replace perfectly good 12-year-old roofs because they're worried about insurance, when the law actually protects them", according to roofing experts.

### What Happens After 15 Years

Once your roof reaches 15 years old, insurance companies can require an inspection before issuing or renewing your policy. However, they still cannot deny coverage based solely on age if an authorized inspector certifies your roof has at least 5 more years of useful life.

## 2026 Legislative Updates: Senate Bill 808

Florida continues to refine its roof insurance laws. Senate Bill 808, which takes effect July 1, 2026, brings several important changes:

### Expanded Inspector Qualifications

The new law expands who can perform roof inspections to include home inspectors licensed under s. 468.8314, building code inspectors certified under s. 468.607, general, building, or residential contractors licensed under s. 489.111 or roofing contractors, professional engineers licensed under s. 471.015, and professional architects licensed under s. 481.213, along with any other individual or entity recognized by the insurer as possessing the necessary qualifications.

### New Roof Type Distinctions

For roofs that are at least 15 years old, insurers must differentiate between a low-slope roof with a 2-inch pitch or less and a steep-slope roof with a pitch of more than 2 inches in their offer for coverage.

## Understanding Roof Age Calculations

For purposes of Florida law, a roof's age is calculated using the last date on which 100 percent of the roof's surface area was built or replaced in accordance with the building code in effect at that time. This means partial replacements don't reset your roof's age until the entire surface has been replaced.

## Insurance Company Practices vs. State Law

While state law provides certain protections, insurance companies may have additional requirements beyond the legal minimums:

### Wind Mitigation Requirements

"Now I have a company who will take your roof if it's over 15 years old, but it has to have a secondary water resistance layer as part of the wind mitigation, and if it doesn't, it's not acceptable. So if you didn't do that 15 years ago, when you got your roof, you can't go to them as an option", explains a Tampa Bay insurance agent.

### Citizens Property Insurance Differences

Citizens Property considers a standard or architectural shingle roof old if it has been in place for 25 years or longer, while slate, tile, clay, metal, or concrete roofs are considered old if they have been in place for 50 years or longer.

## Inspection Requirements and Documentation

### Who Can Inspect Your Roof

Authorized inspectors include:
- Licensed home inspectors
- Certified building code inspectors
- Licensed general, building, or residential contractors
- Licensed roofing contractors
- Professional engineers
- Professional architects

### The 5-Year Useful Life Standard

Insurance companies may not refuse to issue or refuse to renew a homeowner's insurance policy solely because of roof age if an inspection indicates that the roof has 5 years or more of useful life remaining.

## Wind Mitigation and Discounts

### Impact-Resistant Materials

Insurance companies give discounts for metal roofs, and many carriers offer premium credits for homeowners with qualifying metal roofs—meaning your roof investment pays you back in lower insurance bills every single year.

### Documentation Requirements

Many homeowners assume discounts will apply without realizing documentation and a wind mitigation inspection are often required. Metal roofing upgrades must be documented for credit eligibility, and insurers may not apply credits unless formally requested.

## Common Misconceptions and Myths

### The Metal Roof Myth

Florida's statute is completely material-neutral. It doesn't say "metal roofs must be replaced at 15 years." It doesn't single out shingles, tile, or metal. It simply sets a fair rule: if your roof is sound and has life left, insurers cannot automatically cancel you.

### Lifespan Realities

Asphalt shingles typically last 12–20 years in Florida (usually on the low end because of heat, humidity, and storms), while metal roofing lasts 40–70 years, often longer depending on the profile, coating, and installation quality.

## FAQ Section

### Can my insurance be canceled because my roof is old?

Yes, but only if the roof is 15+ years old and does not pass a certified inspection showing sufficient remaining life. For roofs under 15 years old, cancellation based solely on age is prohibited by Florida law.

### How often should I inspect my roof?

Asphalt shingle roofs should be inspected every 2 to 3 years under normal conditions, annually once the roof is 10+ years old, and immediately after any major hurricane, windstorm, or hail event. Tile roofs should be inspected every 3 to 5 years, while metal roofs need inspection every 3 to 5 years, more frequently if you live within a few miles of the coast due to salt corrosion.

### What if I can't find private insurance for my older roof?

If you can't find a private insurer, you might qualify for coverage through Citizens Property Insurance, Florida's state-backed insurance provider. This is often the option for homes with roofs approaching or exceeding typical age limits.

### Will a new roof really save me money on insurance?

Many homeowners find that the savings from a lower insurance premium help offset the cost of roof installation. Additionally, newer roofs result in lower homeowners insurance premiums since they're less risky, which means insurers may offer lower rates, and a new roof adds value to your home if you plan to sell in the future.

### What documentation do I need for wind mitigation credits?

You'll need a current wind mitigation inspection form completed by a qualified inspector, documentation of roof installation or upgrades, and proof of impact-resistant materials or secondary water resistance features.
