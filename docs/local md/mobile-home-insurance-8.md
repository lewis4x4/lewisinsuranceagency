# Mobile Home Insurance in Gainesville, Florida

Mobile homes and manufactured housing provide affordable housing options throughout the Gainesville area. ## Why Mobile Homes Need Specialized Coverage

Mobile homes face different risks than site-built houses. They're more vulnerable to wind damage during severe weather. Construction materials and methods differ from traditional homes. Older units may not meet current tie-down standards or HUD requirements.

Many insurance carriers either avoid mobile homes or price them assuming worst-case scenarios. Lewis Insurance Agency works with carriers who specialize in manufactured housing and offer competitive rates for Alachua County residents.

## What Your Policy Should Cover

A complete mobile home insurance policy includes:

- **Dwelling coverage**: The structure, including any additions or improvements you've made
- **Personal property**: Your belongings inside the home
- **Liability protection**: Coverage if someone is injured on your property
- **Other structures**: Carports, storage buildings, and detached structures
- **Additional living expenses**: Temporary housing if your home becomes uninhabitable

## Factors That Affect Coverage and Pricing

Insurance companies evaluate mobile homes based on:

- **Year manufactured**: Pre-1976 homes were built before HUD construction standards
- **Construction type**: Single-wide, double-wide, or multi-section
- **Tie-down and anchoring**: Proper installation affects wind damage risk
- **Roof condition**: Age, type, and overall condition
- **Location**: Flood zone status, tree proximity, community type

## Mobile Home Communities and Private Land

Coverage requirements and options may differ based on whether your mobile home is in a community or on private land. Community policies may include some shared coverage. Homes on private land may need broader coverage for additional structures and land improvements.
